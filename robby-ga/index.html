Interactive genetic algorithm example

Robby the Robot

Robby is an agent in a grid world. It sees only its current cell and four neighboring cells. A policy maps each local percept to an action, and a genetic algorithm can evolve policies by evaluating average score across many random worlds.

The policy is the genotype: 243 integer genes, one action per percept. Fitness comes from behavior in an environment, not directly from the genome. Good policies are discovered through repeated evaluation, selection, crossover, and mutation.

Section 5

Notes and Interpretation

  • Robby differs from OneMax because fitness is not directly read from the genome.
  • A policy must be tested in an environment, and the same policy can score differently in different random worlds.
  • Averaging over many trials makes fitness more stable.
  • Mutation explores new actions; crossover recombines parts of policies; selection amplifies better average behavior.
  • GA can improve policies, but it is not guaranteed to find an optimal strategy.
  • High fitness depends on reward design, environment distribution, and evaluation settings.
